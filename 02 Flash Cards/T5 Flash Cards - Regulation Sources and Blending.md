# T5 Flash Cards - Regulation Sources and Blending

> Format: Front = Question | Back = Answer

## Regulation Source Recognition

### Card 1
**Front:** What is the main federal drinking-water law for public water systems?
**Back:** Safe Drinking Water Act (SDWA). Source: EPA-SDWA.

### Card 2
**Front:** Which federal regulation part contains National Primary Drinking Water Regulations?
**Back:** 40 CFR Part 141. Source: ECFR-40-CFR-141.

### Card 3
**Front:** Which federal regulation part is associated with state primacy and implementation?
**Back:** 40 CFR Part 142. Source: ECFR-40-CFR-142. Review flag: verify section-level details before final release.

### Card 4
**Front:** Which federal regulation part contains National Secondary Drinking Water Regulations?
**Back:** 40 CFR Part 143. Source: ECFR-40-CFR-143. Review flag: verify section-level details before final release.

### Card 5
**Front:** Where are California drinking-water MCLs and monitoring requirements mainly found?
**Back:** CCR Title 22. Source: CA-LAWBOOK / CA-TITLE-22. Review flag: section-level Title 22 audit still needed.

### Card 6
**Front:** What is the current California source for cross-connection control program requirements?
**Back:** Cross-Connection Control Policy Handbook (CCCPH), which replaced the old Title 17 cross-connection regulations. Source: CA-CCCPH.

### Card 7
**Front:** What rule family covers total coliform, E. coli findings, assessments, and corrective action?
**Back:** Revised Total Coliform Rule / bacteriological monitoring framework. Source: EPA-RTCR / Title 22 context.

### Card 8
**Front:** What rule family covers TTHM and HAA5?
**Back:** Stage 1 and Stage 2 Disinfectants and Disinfection Byproducts Rules. Source: EPA-DBPR.

### Card 9
**Front:** Which rule family covers filtration, turbidity, CT, Giardia, viruses, and Cryptosporidium?
**Back:** Surface Water Treatment Rule family. Source: EPA-SWTR.

### Card 10
**Front:** What regulatory concept is central to lead and copper compliance?
**Back:** Action levels and corrosion-control framework, not a simple finished-water MCL model. Source: EPA-LCR. Review flag: LCRI transition context can change.

## Source-Water Blending

### Card 11
**Front:** What principle is used for source-water blending calculations?
**Back:** Flow-weighted concentration from each source.

### Card 12
**Front:** Source A is 2 MGD at 8 mg/L nitrate and Source B is 1 MGD at 2 mg/L nitrate. What is the blended nitrate?
**Back:** 6 mg/L. Calculation: ((2 x 8) + (1 x 2)) / 3 = 6.

### Card 13
**Front:** A 0.5 MGD source has 12 ug/L arsenic and a 1.5 MGD source has 4 ug/L arsenic. What is the blended arsenic?
**Back:** 6 ug/L. Calculation: ((0.5 x 12) + (1.5 x 4)) / 2.0 = 6.

### Card 14
**Front:** Before changing source-water blend ratios for compliance, what should be checked?
**Back:** Permit/operational limits, expected blended quality, required monitoring, documentation, and supervisor/DDW coordination as applicable.

### Card 15
**Front:** Why can a source blend that lowers nitrate still create an operational concern?
**Back:** It may increase TDS, hardness, DBP formation potential, corrosion, taste/odor, or treatment demand.

### Card 16
**Front:** If a source blend increases chlorine demand, what should the operator evaluate?
**Back:** Disinfectant dose, residual, contact time, affected water, DBP risk, and monitoring/reporting triggers.

### Card 17
**Front:** What is the operator concern if filter turbidity rises after a source blend change?
**Back:** Raw water quality and treatment demand may have changed; check coagulant dose, jar testing, filters, and monitoring requirements.

## Situational Compliance

### Card 18
**Front:** A confirmed E. coli positive routine sample is reported. What is the best operator response?
**Back:** Follow the bacteriological rule response plan, notify required contacts, and coordinate repeat/corrective action requirements.

### Card 19
**Front:** What does a Tier 1 public notice generally indicate?
**Back:** A situation with immediate potential health concern requiring rapid public notice.

### Card 20
**Front:** A compliance sample was analyzed by a lab not accredited for the method. What is the concern?
**Back:** The result may not be acceptable for compliance reporting.
