24. A total coliform positive sample may indicate: A) good disinfectant only B) possible pathway for contamination C) hardness only D) fluoride overdose

29. Consumer Confidence Reports communicate: A) annual water quality information to customers B) chemical invoices C) hydrant painting schedules D) operator timesheets

31. Turbidity performance requirements in filtration are an example of: A) treatment performance control B) fluoridation permit C) service line inventory only D) billing category
32. Acute contaminants are those that can: A) only stain fixtures B) pose health risks from short-term exposure C) only affect taste D) never require notice
33. Nitrate is often treated as an acute concern because it can affect: A) pipe paint B) meter registers C) infants by methemoglobinemia risk D) pump motors
34. Legionella risk is influenced by: A) fluoride feed only B) raw water altitude only C) warm water, stagnation, and poor building water management D) filter sand only
35. Giardia is commonly controlled by: A) billing B) meter replacement C) filtration and disinfection/inactivation D) corrosion coupons only
36. Cryptosporidium is challenging because it is: A) removed by copper only B) resistant to normal chlorination C) created by lime D) measured by pH alone
37. A vulnerability assessment helps identify: A) security and operational weaknesses B) annual leave balances C) pipe thread type D) coagulant color
38. Emergency response planning should include: A) roles, communications, backup resources, and priority actions B) only old phone lists C) no mutual aid D) no exercises
39. A main break can create water quality risk because it may cause: A) lower bacteria always B) depressurization and intrusion C) perfect flushing D) lower water age always
40. After distribution repairs, operators commonly perform: A) no verification B) flushing, disinfection, pressure restoration, and sampling as required C) filter backwash only D) billing only
41. Low distribution residual at a distant site could indicate: A) perfect mixing B) too much CT only C) low risk D) high water age, demand, nitrification, or contamination
42. A customer taste/odor complaint cluster should trigger: A) automatic blame of plumbing only B) no log entry C) pattern review, field checks, and treatment/distribution investigation D) no samples
43. A pressure zone is controlled by: A) operator grade B) elevation, pumps, tanks, and valves C) lab bottle type D) fluoride feed
44. PRVs are used to: A) disinfect tanks B) increase hardness C) reduce/control downstream pressure D) remove arsenic
45. Air relief valves help: A) release trapped air from pipelines B) increase chlorine dose C) remove PFAS D) sample lead
46. A hydrant flushing plan should consider: A) no sample points B) no traffic control ever C) direction, velocity, residual, turbidity, and customer impacts D) only paint color
47. A storage tank overflow event should be assessed for: A) only water waste billing B) sanitary risk, intrusion potential, and operational cause C) no consequence D) pump efficiency only
48. Finished water storage should be protected from: A) sunlight only B) customer notices C) contamination, animals, unauthorized access, and cross-connections D) higher elevation
49. A representative sample is one that: A) is unpreserved regardless of method B) reflects the water quality being evaluated C) is always first water from any tap D) is taken from the easiest hose
