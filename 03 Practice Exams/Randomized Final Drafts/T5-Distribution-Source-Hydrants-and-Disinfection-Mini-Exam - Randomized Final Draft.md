## Questions

1. What is the main purpose of a drinking water distribution system? A) Replace all treatment B) Eliminate all sampling C) Store untreated wastewater D) Deliver potable water to customers while maintaining pressure, flow, and water quality
2. Which list best represents distribution-system components? A) Only lab incubators B) Mains, valves, hydrants, storage, pumps, meters, and services C) Only jar-test paddles D) Only source-water intakes
3. Why are looped distribution systems generally preferred over dead ends? A) They prevent all pipe breaks B) They remove the need for valves C) They improve circulation, redundancy, and operational flexibility D) They eliminate disinfection
4. What water-quality issue is common in dead ends or low-flow areas? A) Elimination of sediment B) No need for flushing C) Increased membrane salt rejection D) Increased water age and residual loss
5. What does AWWA G200 primarily support in this vault? A) Rainfall maps only B) Lab incubation only C) Distribution-system operation and management concepts D) PFAS federal MCL values
6. What is a key caveat for AWWA standards? A) They replace all state rules B) They are quiz sites C) They are not complete specifications and do not supersede law or code D) They prohibit local requirements
7. What are transmission lines? A) Larger pipelines that move water between major system components B) Chlorine tablets only C) Turbidity standards only D) Lab glassware
8. Why does pipe material selection matter? A) It affects pressure capacity, corrosion, installation, water quality, and service life B) It only affects color C) It eliminates maintenance D) It replaces hydraulic design
9. What does pipe C value relate to? A) Coliform results only B) Chloramine breakpoint only C) Calcium hardness only D) Hydraulic roughness/friction in flow calculations
10. What is one purpose of distribution storage? A) Replace all pumps B) Store sewage C) Balance demand and support pressure/emergency supply D) Eliminate residual monitoring
11. Why are valves important in distribution? A) They replace all hydrants B) Isolation, control, flushing, maintenance, and emergency response C) They eliminate all water age D) They are only used in labs
12. What is the primary purpose of a fire hydrant? A) Fluoride analysis B) Incubation C) Public fire protection service D) Chemical coagulation
13. Which hydrant type is intended for freezing climates? A) Lab hydrant B) Wet-only hydrant C) Membrane hydrant D) Dry-barrel fire hydrant
14. Which AWWA standard covers dry-barrel fire hydrants? A) ANSI/AWWA C510 B) ANSI/AWWA C502 C) ANSI/AWWA B112 D) ANSI/AWWA B600
15. Why should non-fire hydrant use be restricted and controlled? A) To avoid valves B) To maintain hydrants for fire emergencies and protect water quality C) To remove chlorine D) To eliminate records
16. If a hydrant cannot be opened or closed by one person using a proper hydrant wrench, what is the best response? A) Remove all hydrants B) Repair it instead of forcing it C) Ignore it D) Force it
17. Why can using a hydrant to dispense drinking water require special care? A) Water never contacts surfaces B) Downstream surfaces may need disinfection and material-suitability evaluation C) Hydrants sterilize automatically D) Hydrants eliminate risk
18. What AWWA manual is commonly paired with hydrant maintenance topics? A) AWWA M17 B) AWWA B403 C) AWWA B600 D) AWWA B114
19. Why is distribution-system disinfection important? A) It removes treatment B) Distribution deficiencies can contribute to contamination risk C) It replaces pressure D) It eliminates sampling
20. What is a common contamination source during water main installation or repair? A) Spreadsheet formatting B) Soil, sediment, trench water, or foreign water entering pipe/appurtenances C) UV serial number D) CCR wording
21. Which sequence best fits pipeline disinfection work? A) Inspection, sanitary construction, flushing/cleaning, disinfection, final flushing, bacteriological testing, connection, documentation B) Jar test only C) Skip inspection D) Paint pipe only
22. What are common storage-facility contamination routes? A) pH meters only B) Clean sample bottles only C) Sealed hatches only D) Open/missing vents, hatches, roof openings, inspection activities, and foreign matter
23. Which AWWA standards are commonly tied to water main and storage disinfection? A) B112 and B114 only B) C651 and C652 C) B600 and B604 only D) C502 and C510 only
24. Why is bacteriological testing part of disinfection work? A) It confirms paint color B) It measures pipe C value C) It helps verify sanitary conditions before return to potable service D) It replaces disinfection
25. Why may dechlorination be needed after disinfection? A) Highly chlorinated water may need treatment before discharge B) It replaces sampling C) Chlorine disappears instantly D) It increases pipe roughness
26. Which chemicals are commonly used for pipeline/storage disinfection? A) Only carbon B) Liquid chlorine, sodium hypochlorite, and calcium hypochlorite C) Only alum D) Only table salt
27. Which free-chlorine species is more effective as a disinfectant? A) Hypochlorite ion B) Nitrate C) Hypochlorous acid D) Calcium carbonate
28. What happens to chlorine effectiveness as pH rises? A) More free chlorine shifts toward less-effective hypochlorite ion B) Chlorine becomes turbidity C) Chlorine becomes more acidic D) Chlorine becomes PFAS
29. What does AWWA M20 support? A) Only jar-test math B) Only wells C) Chlorination/chloramination practice, chemistry, equipment, safety, strategy, and residual testing D) Only fire hose threads
30. What does AWWA B403 cover? A) Fire hydrants B) Cross-connection assemblies C) Aluminum sulfate D) Membrane systems
31. What is aluminum sulfate commonly used for? A) Fire-flow testing only B) Backflow testing only C) Hydrant operation D) Coagulation
32. What does AWWA B600 cover? A) RO/NF systems B) Dry-barrel hydrants C) Aluminum sulfate D) Powdered activated carbon
33. What does AWWA B604 cover? A) PFAS timing only B) Granular activated carbon C) Program management only D) Hydrant color only
34. What is activated carbon commonly used for? A) Raising all residuals B) Replacing hydrants C) Adsorbing taste-and-odor compounds and some organic contaminants D) Measuring pipe wall thickness
35. What does AWWA B112 cover? A) Double-check assemblies B) Microfiltration and ultrafiltration membrane systems C) Dry-barrel hydrants D) Aluminum sulfate
36. What does AWWA B114 cover? A) Hydrant color only B) Water main disinfection only C) Powdered carbon only D) Reverse osmosis and nanofiltration systems
37. Which membrane group generally removes dissolved material better? A) MF/UF B) Fire hydrants C) RO/NF D) Dead-end mains
38. Why is the uploaded Water Sources file caveated? A) It is blank B) It has valid AWWA front matter but also suspicious non-book/unrelated parsed text C) It is only a hydrant standard D) It is official California regulation
39. Product standards should be paired with operator/training references because: A) Site conditions never matter B) Product standards are never useful C) Product standards do not fully explain operations, troubleshooting, or site-specific design D) Operator references override all laws
40. What controls final installation, operation, and compliance decisions? A) Current regulations, state/local rules, authority having jurisdiction, engineering design, manufacturer instructions, and utility SOPs B) A single old workbook only C) Flashcards only D) The shortest answer choice
