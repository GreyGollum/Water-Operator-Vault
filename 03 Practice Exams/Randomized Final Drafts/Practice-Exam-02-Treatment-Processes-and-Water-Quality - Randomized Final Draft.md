21. Packed tower aeration increases contact between: A) coagulant and sludge B) resin and brine C) fluoride and lime D) air and water
22. Taste and odor compounds are often addressed with: A) PAC/GAC, oxidation, source control B) meter replacement C) higher billing rates D) pressure gauges only
23. Ozone is a strong oxidant but can form: A) nitrate always B) copper pipe C) lead scale D) bromate when bromide is present
24. UV disinfection effectiveness depends on: A) only tank color B) hardness only C) fluoride feed D) UV dose, transmittance, lamp condition, and flow
25. Chlorine dioxide can create: A) pump headloss B) lead action levels C) sand carryover only D) chlorite/chlorate concerns
26. Free ammonia with chlorine can contribute to: A) nitrification risk in chloraminated systems B) lower bacteria always C) less monitoring D) lower water age
27. Nitrification in distribution can cause: A) stable residual only B) loss of chloramine residual and nitrite/nitrate issues C) lower HPC always D) no pH change ever
28. Water age is important because it can affect: A) pipe diameter immediately B) certified operator grade C) residual decay and DBP formation D) elevation datum
29. Finished-water pH is important for: A) flow unit labels B) employee schedules C) corrosion control and disinfectant performance D) hydrant color
30. Alkalinity acts as: A) bacterial indicator B) pH buffering capacity C) taste panel D) pump speed
31. Hardness is mainly associated with: A) lead and copper only B) calcium and magnesium C) ozone and bromide D) chlorine and ammonia
32. Softening may be used to reduce: A) hardness B) color from logs only C) viruses only D) DBPs only
33. Lime softening can raise: A) PFAS B) chlorine demand always C) nitrate D) pH
34. Corrosion control commonly uses: A) less monitoring B) pH/alkalinity adjustment or corrosion inhibitors C) larger sample bottles only D) more turbidity
35. Orthophosphate is often used to: A) disinfect wells B) regenerate resin C) form protective corrosion-control films D) backwash filters
36. A sudden increase in chlorine demand may indicate: A) lower risk B) clean analyzers only C) organic loading, ammonia, contamination, or process change D) lower source water reactivity
37. Color in raw water can be caused by: A) fluoridation only B) natural organic matter, metals, or algae C) pipe maps D) pressure gauges
38. Algae can affect treatment by: A) lowering coagulant need always B) eliminating DBPs C) improving all filters D) causing taste/odor, filter clogging, toxins, and pH swings
39. Cyanotoxin response should include: A) only increasing pump speed B) source monitoring, treatment evaluation, and regulatory coordination C) ignoring blooms D) stopping records
40. Turbidity standards for filtered water are linked to: A) employee hours B) pathogen removal performance C) customer class D) service meter age
41. A clearwell provides: A) accounting B) raw water screening C) storage and contact time D) coagulant only
42. Baffling improves CT by: A) lowering disinfectant strength always B) increasing raw turbidity C) removing PFAS D) reducing short-circuiting
43. T10 is used in CT calculations to estimate: A) pump horsepower B) effective contact time C) sludge age D) tank height only
44. Higher flow through a contact basin usually: A) has no effect B) increases detention time C) eliminates need for residual D) reduces detention/contact time
45. A low clearwell level may reduce: A) plant voltage B) finished hardness C) raw water algae D) available contact time and storage reserve
46. The best response to failing CT is to: A) disable analyzer B) ignore if water looks clear C) evaluate flow, residual, pH, temperature, and contact time; correct before delivery D) write it down later only
47. Turbidity, chlorine residual, pH, and temperature matter for: A) hydrant color B) billing C) disinfection compliance evaluation D) staffing schedule only
48. A chemical day tank should have: A) no secondary containment ever B) no labels C) open top only D) labeling, containment, and safe handling controls
49. Sodium hypochlorite degrades faster with: A) darkness and cool storage B) lower age only C) heat, sunlight, age, and high concentration D) no air exposure only
