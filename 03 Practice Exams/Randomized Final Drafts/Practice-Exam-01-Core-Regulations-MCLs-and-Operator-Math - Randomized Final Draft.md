## Questions

1. The federal law that gives EPA authority to set national drinking water standards is: A) CERCLA B) RCRA C) Clean Water Act D) Safe Drinking Water Act
2. A California T5 treatment operator is most associated with: A) wastewater collection B) laboratory-only work C) entry-level plant operation D) advanced/chief-level treatment responsibility
3. Primary drinking water standards are mainly designed to protect: A) pipe color B) taste and odor C) billing accuracy D) public health
4. Secondary drinking water standards are mainly related to: A) operator certification B) health-based MCLs C) aesthetics such as taste, odor, color, and staining D) source capacity
5. The MCL for nitrate as nitrogen is: A) 10 mg/L B) 1 mg/L C) 45 mg/L D) 5 mg/L
6. The MCL for nitrite as nitrogen is: A) 50 mg/L B) 1 mg/L C) 10 mg/L D) 0.1 mg/L
7. The action level for lead under lead and copper rules is commonly expressed as: A) 0.050 mg/L B) 0.005 mg/L C) 0.015 mg/L D) 0.010 mg/L
8. The action level for copper is commonly expressed as: A) 0.015 mg/L B) 13 mg/L C) 0.13 mg/L D) 1.3 mg/L
9. The federal MCL for arsenic is: A) 0.050 mg/L B) 0.010 mg/L C) 0.005 mg/L D) 1.0 mg/L
10. The MCL for TTHM is: A) 0.060 mg/L B) 0.010 mg/L C) 0.080 mg/L D) 0.100 mg/L
11. The MCL for HAA5 is: A) 0.040 mg/L B) 0.080 mg/L C) 0.060 mg/L D) 1.0 mg/L
12. A sample collected after water has remained motionless in premise plumbing is especially associated with: A) jar testing B) well yield testing C) turbidity profiling D) lead and copper sampling
13. CT in disinfection means: A) chemical transfer B) concentration times contact time C) chlorine tested D) corrosion trend
14. The basic chemical feed equation for pounds per day is: A) mg/L ÷ 8.34 B) mg/L x MGD x 8.34 C) gpm x psi D) ft x ft x ft
15. A plant treats 4.0 MGD at 2.5 mg/L chlorine. Approximate chlorine feed is: A) 8.3 lb/day B) 83.4 lb/day C) 166.8 lb/day D) 41.7 lb/day
16. A 2 MG basin has 1.5 MG usable volume. Flow is 3 MGD. The theoretical detention time is: A) 12 hr B) 24 hr C) 0.25 hr D) 0.5 hr
17. A flow of 1 MGD is approximately: A) 8.34 gpm B) 70 gpm C) 694 gpm D) 1,440 gpm
18. One cubic foot of water contains approximately: A) 3.14 gallons B) 7.48 gallons C) 1 gallon D) 62.4 gallons
19. Water weighs approximately: A) 2.31 lb/gal B) 62.4 lb/gal C) 7.48 lb/gal D) 8.34 lb/gal
20. A pressure of 1 psi equals approximately: A) 7.48 ft of head B) 8.34 ft of head C) 0.433 ft of head D) 2.31 ft of head
21. The organism most resistant to normal chlorine disinfection is generally: A) Giardia B) E. coli C) total coliform D) Cryptosporidium
22. Conventional filtration includes: A) fluoridation only B) aeration only C) coagulation, flocculation, sedimentation, filtration D) softening only
23. Coagulation is primarily used to: A) raise nitrate B) increase DBPs C) add hardness D) destabilize particles
24. Flocculation is primarily used to: A) gently mix particles into larger floc B) destroy viruses C) disinfect storage tanks D) measure chlorine
25. Sedimentation removes particles mainly by: A) reverse osmosis B) gravity settling C) ion exchange D) UV intensity
26. A high settled-water turbidity leaving sedimentation often increases loading on: A) distribution maps B) billing software C) fluoride tanks D) filters
27. A sudden filter effluent turbidity spike can indicate: A) lower demand B) filter breakthrough C) lower particle loading D) perfect coagulation
28. The main purpose of filter backwashing is to: A) raise alkalinity B) reduce pump speed C) add chlorine D) remove accumulated solids and restore headloss
29. Disinfection byproducts generally increase when disinfectant reacts with: A) inert sand B) sodium chloride only C) nitrogen gas D) natural organic matter
30. Chloramination is commonly used to: A) replace filtration B) eliminate corrosion C) remove all PFAS D) maintain longer-lasting residual
31. Free chlorine residual is usually more reactive than: A) dissolved oxygen B) chloramine residual C) hardness D) alkalinity
32. A cross-connection program is intended to prevent: A) backflow and contamination B) overbilling C) hydrant painting defects D) pump cavitation only
33. Backpressure backflow can occur when downstream pressure is: A) higher than supply pressure B) unrelated to pressure C) lower than supply pressure D) zero
34. Backsiphonage is commonly associated with: A) high hardness B) excessive source nitrate C) negative or reduced supply pressure D) filter ripening
35. A boil water notice is most directly related to: A) pump efficiency B) high electric use C) low aesthetic color only D) possible microbial risk
36. A loss of positive pressure in distribution is concerning because it can: A) reduce water age only B) remove DBPs C) allow intrusion D) improve CT
37. The Revised Total Coliform Rule uses coliform detections to trigger: A) assessment and corrective action B) filter-to-waste only C) meter replacement D) fluoride feed
38. E. coli detection in a routine bacteriological sample is generally more serious than total coliform alone because it indicates: A) fecal contamination potential B) high hardness C) low alkalinity D) scaling
39. The best first action after a confirmed critical analyzer alarm is to: A) increase all feeds blindly B) ignore it until shift change C) disable SCADA alarms D) verify the reading and assess process impact
40. Jar testing is used to evaluate: A) meter reading B) staffing level C) coagulation dose and treatment response D) well casing depth
41. Alkalinity is important in coagulation because it affects: A) pH buffering and chemical reactions B) motor voltage only C) sample bottle color D) pipe diameter only
42. Low pH can increase risk of: A) nitrate formation B) pipe enlargement C) turbidity disappearance D) corrosion
43. High pH can reduce effectiveness of: A) free chlorine disinfection B) pipe labels C) SCADA screenshots D) meter routes
44. PFAS treatment options commonly include: A) GAC, ion exchange, RO B) aeration only C) fluoridation D) sedimentation only
45. Granular activated carbon removes contaminants mainly by: A) bacteriological growth B) adsorption C) pH neutralization only D) gravity alone
46. Ion exchange removes target ions by: A) adding sand B) exchanging ions on a resin C) raising temperature D) ultraviolet light
47. Reverse osmosis removes many dissolved contaminants by: A) pressure-driven membrane separation B) open-channel settling C) chlorine demand D) aeration turbulence
48. A pump losing prime is most directly a problem with: A) fluoride MCL B) high finished water chlorine C) suction/air leakage or insufficient liquid D) jar-test color
49. Cavitation is associated with: A) normal lubrication only B) vapor bubbles forming/collapsing in pump zones C) high fluoride D) stable suction conditions
50. A good operator log entry should be: A) vague B) clear, timely, factual, and traceable C) written days later D) opinion-only
