## Questions

1. For drinking-water compliance monitoring, which source has the highest authority? A) Manufacturer catalog B) Informal training handout C) Current CFR and incorporated approved method text D) Operator study note
2. Under routine RTCR total coliform/E. coli monitoring, the standard sample volume is usually: A) 100 mL B) 10 L C) 1 mL D) 500 mL
3. For RTCR routine samples, presence/absence testing is generally: A) Only used for turbidity B) Never allowed C) Sufficient for total coliform/E. coli compliance D) Used only for metals
4. Why is sodium thiosulfate used in microbiology sample bottles for chlorinated water? A) To add nutrients for algae B) To preserve metals C) To neutralize chlorine residual D) To increase pH to 14
5. A collection-to-incubation time beyond the approved holding window can: A) Improve sample reliability B) Remove the need for documentation C) Convert CFU to MPN automatically D) Make a microbiological result questionable or invalid
6. CFU stands for: A) Carbon filtration units B) Chlorine formation units C) Certified fluoride uptake D) Colony-forming units
7. MPN stands for: A) Minimum particle number B) Meter probe normalization C) Most probable number D) Maximum pressure neutralization
8. HPC is best described as: A) A chlorine dose calculation B) A metal digestion method C) A general heterotrophic bacterial growth indicator D) The same thing as an E. coli MCL
9. Which is an HPC method family captured in the lab section? A) Pour plate / plate count B) Ion exchange softening C) Lime-soda coagulation D) Fluoride MCL averaging
10. Precision describes: A) The sample bottle size B) Closeness to the true value only C) Reproducibility of repeated results D) The age of the instrument
11. Accuracy describes: A) How many samples are shipped B) The color of a reagent C) Closeness to the true or accepted value D) Whether a sample is groundwater
12. Can results be precise but inaccurate? A) Only for turbidity B) No C) Only if pH is 7 D) Yes
13. A blank is used mainly to check: A) Contamination or background contribution B) Distribution pressure C) Pump efficiency D) Filter loading rate
14. A duplicate or replicate analysis is used mainly to check: A) Lead service line inventory B) CT table compliance C) Tank overflow elevation D) Precision/repeatability
15. A spike or known addition is used mainly to check: A) Floc blanket depth B) Pipe age C) Accuracy/recovery D) Valve direction
16. Chain of custody is important because it: A) Raises chlorine residual B) Preserves sample traceability C) Lowers turbidity D) Replaces calibration
17. An analytical standard is used to: A) Clean the sink drain B) Store waste acid C) Calibrate or check a method D) Increase sample hold time
18. Reagents should generally not be used past expiration because: A) Strength or purity may change B) They become official regulations C) They always become harmless D) They stop needing labels
19. Reagent water quality matters because: A) It replaces QA/QC B) It only affects taste panels C) It can add background or interfere with analyses D) It is used only for fire protection
20. The difference between concentration and acid/base strength is that: A) They are identical terms B) Strength means bottle size C) Concentration means sample temperature only D) Concentration is amount present; strength is degree of ionization
21. Acids and bases should be stored separately because: A) They become more accurate together B) They can react and release heat C) They cancel documentation requirements D) They are both sample preservatives only
22. Oxidizers and reducers should be stored separately because: A) They form perfect blanks B) They lower sample temperature C) They replace fume hoods D) They can react vigorously
23. Beaker graduations are generally: A) Always NIST-certified B) Used as legal standards C) Approximate D) More accurate than Class A pipets
24. A buret is commonly used to: A) Deliver measured titrant in titration B) Store chlorine gas C) Incubate microbiology samples D) Measure pipe flow directly
25. A meniscus reading error affects: A) Filter media size only B) Volume measurement C) Pump horsepower only D) Operator certification only
26. Analytical balances should be protected from drafts and vibration because: A) Vibration improves accuracy B) These can affect mass readings C) Drafts sterilize samples D) Balances are only decorative
27. A titration endpoint is: A) The last sample of the month B) The highest tank level C) The method-defined point where the reaction is considered complete D) The instrument serial number
28. A colorimetric test generally estimates concentration by: A) Pipe diameter B) Color intensity from a chemical reaction C) Pump speed D) Valve torque
29. A turbidimetric method is based on: A) Valve position B) Total organic carbon only C) Cloudiness or light scatter D) Taste threshold
30. pH and ion-selective electrodes require: A) Proper calibration and stable readings B) Chlorine gas feed C) No standards ever D) Hot lime softening
31. Hardness is commonly associated with which method family in operator training? A) Cryptosporidium microscopy only B) UV disinfection only C) EDTA titration D) Ozone CT only
32. Alkalinity is commonly associated with: A) Backflow device testing only B) Titration-based methods C) Virus enumeration only D) Distribution flushing only
33. Residual chlorine testing may use: A) Only a balance B) DPD colorimetric methods or amperometric titration, depending on approved context C) Only a microscope D) Only a jar test paddle
34. Turbidity is commonly measured by: A) Nephelometric methods B) Colony counts C) EDTA hardness titration D) Sodium thiosulfate blanks only
35. Dissolved oxygen in older wet-chemistry training is associated with: A) Winkler method concepts B) Fluoride secondary standard only C) PFAS hazard index only D) Lead and copper tap sampling only
36. Nitrate/nitrite and phosphate are often associated with: A) Colorimetric nutrient method families B) Backwash rate only C) Pressure-zone maps only D) Valve exercising
37. A certified laboratory SOP should be treated as: A) Required operating direction for that lab/method B) Less important than a quiz card C) Optional decoration D) A substitute for sample labeling
38. If an unusual lab result is received, the operator should first: A) Dispose of all records B) Change the result to expected value C) Verify sample identity/result status and follow SOP/supervisor/regulatory path D) Ignore it until next month
39. Why should lab method versions matter? A) Versions only affect font size B) Versions replace QA/QC C) Versions never matter D) Approval can depend on exact method version/date
40. What should be done before using a training-reference method as a compliance method? A) Assume it is approved forever B) Crosscheck against CFR/EPA/state approved methods C) Remove the method number D) Skip certification requirements
41. The WEF lab techniques reference is best used as: A) The controlling drinking-water legal authority B) A training/reference source for lab fundamentals C) A substitute for SDS D) A replacement for 40 CFR Part 141
42. The Water and Wastewater Examination Manual is useful mainly for: A) Method-family awareness and QA/QC training B) Replacing Standard Methods entirely C) Setting PFAS MCLs D) Current California legal text
43. The uploaded Water Quality Concepts files should be used carefully because: A) File-integrity issues were observed B) They contain no sampling topics C) They replace all EPA methods D) They are official California Title 22 text
44. Which item belongs in lab QA/QC records? A) Calibration/check results B) Pump paint color C) Operator lunch schedule only D) Tree trimming schedule
45. What is a common reason for using certified standards or certificates of analysis? A) To avoid calibration B) To eliminate hold times C) To prove a sample was not collected D) Traceability and known quality
46. What is a key reason not to copy full method procedures into the vault? A) Method names are unimportant B) Operators cannot learn concepts C) Copyright/SOP concerns and need to keep study notes at operator-summary level D) Flashcards require legal text only
47. Which source layer is strongest for drinking-water compliance method approval? A) Unverified quiz site B) Social media summary C) CFR / incorporated approved method D) A handwritten shortcut with no source
48. What does “method recognition” mean in this vault? A) Memorizing every procedural step verbatim B) Ignoring approved-method versions C) Knowing what a method family is used for and its limits without replacing SOPs D) Using any kit for any analyte
49. Why should water and wastewater method cards be tagged carefully? A) Drinking-water samples never need labs B) Some methods/authorities differ between drinking-water compliance and wastewater permit testing C) Wastewater methods automatically set drinking-water MCLs D) Tags are only decorative
50. Before public release, lab-method cards should be: A) Published without review B) Crosschecked against EPA/CFR/state sources and caveated where needed C) Stripped of source IDs D) Mixed with unsourced exam dumps
