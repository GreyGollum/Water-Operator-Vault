25. Instrument calibration records should document: A) next vacation B) date, standard, result, adjustment, and person C) opinions only D) nothing if passed
26. A failed chlorine analyzer should be handled by: A) ignoring residual B) verification, repair/calibration, and backup monitoring C) no sample collection D) disabling disinfection
27. Redundancy in critical systems means: A) no spare parts B) backup equipment or alternate path exists C) fewer safeguards D) single point failure only

30. Chemical PPE is selected based on: A) weather only B) chemical hazard and SDS C) operator preference only D) pipe diameter
31. SDS information helps identify: A) hazards, handling, storage, PPE, and response B) jar test dose only C) consumer confidence rules D) pump curves
32. Secondary containment is used to: A) contain spills/leaks B) lower CT C) raise residual D) flush mains
33. A chlorine gas leak response should emphasize: A) ignoring odor B) untrained entry C) life safety, alarms, evacuation/isolation, and trained response D) opening all doors blindly
34. Hypochlorite and acid contact can release: A) chlorine gas B) harmless sand C) nitrate D) oxygen only
35. Hypochlorite and ammonia mixing can form: A) manganese B) lead scale only C) chloramines and potentially hazardous vapors D) arsenic
36. Preventive maintenance is intended to: A) replace records B) increase emergency repairs C) avoid inspections D) reduce failure likelihood and extend equipment life
37. A critical spare-parts list should focus on: A) parts that would limit safe production if unavailable B) random bolts only C) decorative items D) expired chemicals
38. Root cause analysis asks: A) what color alarm was B) how to hide it C) why the failure happened and how recurrence can be prevented D) who to blame only
39. A near-miss report is valuable because it: A) eliminates PPE B) identifies hazards before injury or failure occurs C) replaces training D) proves nothing happened

41. Standard operating procedures should be: A) never revised B) current, accessible, and used consistently C) verbal only D) secret
42. A valve closed in the wrong position can cause: A) perfect mixing B) no impact ever C) lower all risk D) pressure, flow, or water quality problems

46. Electrical work should be performed by: A) anyone during shift B) only if dry floor exists C) untrained staff D) qualified personnel following safety procedures
