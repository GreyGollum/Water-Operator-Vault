## Questions

1. A plant has rising raw turbidity, falling alkalinity, and poorer settled-water turbidity after a storm. Best first operating focus? A) reassess coagulation chemistry and jar-test/adjust dose and pH control B) lower all chemical feed C) stop all monitoring D) ignore because filters will fix it
2. Finished water turbidity is still below the regulatory maximum but trending upward across multiple filters. Best response? A) disable alarms B) only notify customers C) investigate before a limit is reached D) wait for a violation
3. One filter shows turbidity spikes after every backwash while others do not. Most likely review area? A) that filter's backwash/ripening/media/underdrain condition B) CCR mailing list C) source nitrate only D) tank altitude
4. Coagulant dose is increased and settled turbidity improves, but finished pH drops near the lower corrosion-control target. Best next step? A) ignore pH B) keep increasing dose only C) balance coagulation with alkalinity/pH control D) stop corrosion monitoring
5. A chlorine analyzer shows low residual, but grab samples at the same point are normal. Best action? A) shut down all treatment B) increase chlorine until analyzer rises C) assume water is unsafe without verification D) troubleshoot analyzer while maintaining verified manual checks
6. A low clearwell level during peak demand threatens CT. Best operational concern? A) contact time may be reduced and compliance margin may shrink B) pH always rises C) water gets heavier D) turbidity always drops
7. If CT is failing, the operator should evaluate: A) residual, T10/contact time, pH, temperature, and flow B) only payroll C) only fluoride feed D) only tank paint
8. A source has PFAS detections above an applicable response level. Best system response? A) chlorinate harder B) coordinate with regulator, evaluate treatment/blending/source removal, and handle required notification C) flush dead ends only D) ignore until customers ask
9. A PFAS sample may be invalid if: A) it was cold B) it was labeled C) sampling contamination or improper materials compromised the sample D) it was analyzed by LC-MS/MS
10. A new source has lower PFAS but higher manganese. Best T5-level thinking? A) ignore manganese B) switch immediately without evaluation C) compare whole-water-quality impacts, treatment needs, compliance, and customer effects D) choose based only on distance
11. A main break causes low pressure and muddy water complaints. Best priority? A) restore aesthetics only B) assume no intrusion C) delay records D) protect public health: isolate, repair, flush, disinfect/sample, and notify as required
12. A storage tank hatch is found unsecured. Best response? A) assess sanitary breach, secure site, coordinate sampling/disinfection/notification as needed B) close it and say nothing C) increase fluoride D) repaint tank
13. A distribution residual is low at a far dead end. Possible causes include: A) water age, demand, nitrification, biofilm, or inadequate turnover B) lower risk C) better disinfection D) lower customer count only
14. Chloraminated system shows falling total chlorine, rising nitrite, and low free ammonia control. Suspect: A) arsenic precipitation B) filter ripening C) PFAS breakthrough D) nitrification
15. A nitrification response plan may include: A) no samples B) stopping all chlorine C) increasing water age D) flushing, residual management, ammonia control, storage turnover, and monitoring
16. DBP results are high in distant parts of the system. Likely contributing factor? A) too little contact time only B) no organic matter ever C) high water age and precursor/residual interaction D) pump cavitation
17. Reducing chlorine to control DBPs can create risk if it: A) removes organics B) eliminates sampling C) compromises microbial protection D) lowers all risk
18. Enhanced coagulation is used primarily to: A) reduce lead service lines directly B) increase pump head C) disinfect tanks D) improve removal of DBP precursors
19. Algae bloom in reservoir creates taste/odor complaints. Best response? A) turn off filters B) billing adjustment only C) no action D) source monitoring plus treatment adjustment such as oxidation/adsorption/coagulation strategy
20. Cyanotoxin concern should trigger: A) lower sampling B) no treatment review C) monitoring and public-health/regulatory coordination D) routine odor note only
21. A chemical delivery arrives for the wrong tank connection. Best action? A) stop unloading until chemical/tank/connection are verified B) unload quickly C) let driver decide alone D) dilute with water
22. Hypochlorite mixed with acid can release: A) oxygen only B) chlorine gas C) fluoride D) sand
23. A caustic feed pump sticks on and pH rises rapidly. Best response? A) isolate/control feed, verify pH, protect treatment/distribution, and document B) disable pH analyzer C) ignore if water is clear D) add acid blindly without procedure
24. A coagulant feed failure during high turbidity can quickly affect: A) clarification/filter performance and finished water turbidity B) fluoride MCL C) tank coating D) customer billing only
25. A filter is producing acceptable turbidity but particle counts are rising. Best T5 interpretation? A) early warning may indicate performance change before turbidity limit B) filter is perfect C) no need to investigate D) particle counts never matter
26. A membrane integrity failure indicates: A) potential barrier compromise B) more alkalinity C) lower PFAS D) stronger chlorine
27. RO treatment for PFAS requires planning for: A) hydrant flushing B) concentrate handling C) jar-test paddles D) chlorine residual only
28. GAC breakthrough means: A) tank has air gap B) contaminant is passing through as media capacity is exhausted C) pH is too low only D) media is brand new
29. Ion exchange resin exhaustion can cause: A) lower water age B) instant disinfection C) target contaminant leakage/breakthrough D) more CT
30. A lab reports an unexpected contaminant detection just above an MCL. First step? A) publicly announce without review B) ignore unless customer complains C) delete sample D) verify result, sample details, compliance basis, and required response
31. A monitoring violation means: A) pump failed B) required monitoring/reporting was missed or invalid C) contaminant always exceeded health limit D) source dried up
32. A treatment technique violation means: A) only a late bill B) required process/control was not met C) only a customer complaint D) all water is automatically safe
33. Public notification timing depends mainly on: A) weather B) operator preference C) tank size D) risk/violation tier and regulatory requirements
34. A Tier 1 situation generally indicates: A) no health concern B) annual routine notice only C) billing update D) urgent potential health risk requiring rapid notice
35. A Consumer Confidence Report is: A) daily pump log B) chemical SDS C) filter inspection sheet D) annual customer water quality report
36. A SCADA trend shows pump amps slowly increasing over weeks. Best use of this trend? A) increase chlorine B) delete history C) ignore until trip D) plan inspection/maintenance before failure
37. Pump cavitation can damage: A) coagulant only B) impellers and pump components C) CCRs D) sample bottles
38. Water hammer can be reduced by: A) ignoring air valves B) faster valve closures C) controlled valve operation, surge protection, and pump control D) lowering all residual
39. Lockout/tagout protects workers from: A) taste and odor B) microplastics C) nitrate D) hazardous energy
40. Confined-space entry should occur only after: A) no paperwork B) lone entry C) hazard evaluation, atmospheric testing, controls, and permit/procedure as applicable D) a quick look
41. A good emergency response plan includes: A) only old phone numbers B) no public communication C) no maps D) roles, contacts, alternatives, communication, and exercises
42. Mutual aid is useful because it can provide: A) no documentation B) staff, equipment, supplies, or operational support during emergencies C) automatic MCL waivers D) lower DBPs directly
43. A vulnerability assessment helps identify: A) filter loading only B) finished pH C) fluoride dose D) security and operational weaknesses
44. Backflow risk is highest where: A) tanks are full B) a cross-connection exists with pressure reversal potential C) no plumbing exists D) only clean water exists
45. An air gap protects by: A) physical separation between potable and nonpotable sources B) pH buffering C) UV light D) chemical reaction
46. Lead and copper compliance sampling focuses on: A) customer taps/premise plumbing under specified conditions B) hydrants only C) filter backwash only D) raw water only
47. Corrosion control changes can affect: A) only pump horsepower B) only source yield C) lead/copper release and distribution stability D) only tank level
48. If a process change improves turbidity but increases DBP formation potential, best response? A) stop monitoring B) optimize the full treatment train rather than one metric alone C) keep change forever D) ignore DBPs
49. T5-level leadership means: A) only doing single calculations B) avoiding communication C) waiting for others only D) anticipating system-wide consequences and directing safe, compliant response
50. In a serious treatment upset, the final priority should be: A) production convenience B) public health and compliance C) avoiding paperwork D) keeping alarms quiet
