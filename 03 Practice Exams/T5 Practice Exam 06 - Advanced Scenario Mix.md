# T5 Practice Exam 06 - Advanced Scenario Mix

> A tougher mixed scenario set for [[T5 Study Hub]]. This is meant to feel more like T5-level judgment: process control, compliance, troubleshooting, risk ranking, and operations leadership.

## Questions

1. A plant has rising raw turbidity, falling alkalinity, and poorer settled-water turbidity after a storm. Best first operating focus? A) reassess coagulation chemistry and jar-test/adjust dose and pH control B) ignore because filters will fix it C) lower all chemical feed D) stop all monitoring
2. Finished water turbidity is still below the regulatory maximum but trending upward across multiple filters. Best response? A) investigate before a limit is reached B) wait for a violation C) disable alarms D) only notify customers
3. One filter shows turbidity spikes after every backwash while others do not. Most likely review area? A) that filter's backwash/ripening/media/underdrain condition B) source nitrate only C) CCR mailing list D) tank altitude
4. Coagulant dose is increased and settled turbidity improves, but finished pH drops near the lower corrosion-control target. Best next step? A) balance coagulation with alkalinity/pH control B) keep increasing dose only C) ignore pH D) stop corrosion monitoring
5. A chlorine analyzer shows low residual, but grab samples at the same point are normal. Best action? A) troubleshoot analyzer while maintaining verified manual checks B) increase chlorine until analyzer rises C) shut down all treatment D) assume water is unsafe without verification
6. A low clearwell level during peak demand threatens CT. Best operational concern? A) contact time may be reduced and compliance margin may shrink B) water gets heavier C) pH always rises D) turbidity always drops
7. If CT is failing, the operator should evaluate: A) residual, T10/contact time, pH, temperature, and flow B) only tank paint C) only fluoride feed D) only payroll
8. A source has PFAS detections above an applicable response level. Best system response? A) coordinate with regulator, evaluate treatment/blending/source removal, and handle required notification B) ignore until customers ask C) chlorinate harder D) flush dead ends only
9. A PFAS sample may be invalid if: A) sampling contamination or improper materials compromised the sample B) it was cold C) it was labeled D) it was analyzed by LC-MS/MS
10. A new source has lower PFAS but higher manganese. Best T5-level thinking? A) compare whole-water-quality impacts, treatment needs, compliance, and customer effects B) switch immediately without evaluation C) ignore manganese D) choose based only on distance
11. A main break causes low pressure and muddy water complaints. Best priority? A) protect public health: isolate, repair, flush, disinfect/sample, and notify as required B) restore aesthetics only C) delay records D) assume no intrusion
12. A storage tank hatch is found unsecured. Best response? A) assess sanitary breach, secure site, coordinate sampling/disinfection/notification as needed B) close it and say nothing C) repaint tank D) increase fluoride
13. A distribution residual is low at a far dead end. Possible causes include: A) water age, demand, nitrification, biofilm, or inadequate turnover B) lower customer count only C) better disinfection D) lower risk
14. Chloraminated system shows falling total chlorine, rising nitrite, and low free ammonia control. Suspect: A) nitrification B) arsenic precipitation C) PFAS breakthrough D) filter ripening
15. A nitrification response plan may include: A) flushing, residual management, ammonia control, storage turnover, and monitoring B) stopping all chlorine C) increasing water age D) no samples
16. DBP results are high in distant parts of the system. Likely contributing factor? A) high water age and precursor/residual interaction B) too little contact time only C) no organic matter ever D) pump cavitation
17. Reducing chlorine to control DBPs can create risk if it: A) compromises microbial protection B) lowers all risk C) removes organics D) eliminates sampling
18. Enhanced coagulation is used primarily to: A) improve removal of DBP precursors B) increase pump head C) disinfect tanks D) reduce lead service lines directly
19. Algae bloom in reservoir creates taste/odor complaints. Best response? A) source monitoring plus treatment adjustment such as oxidation/adsorption/coagulation strategy B) no action C) billing adjustment only D) turn off filters
20. Cyanotoxin concern should trigger: A) monitoring and public-health/regulatory coordination B) routine odor note only C) lower sampling D) no treatment review
21. A chemical delivery arrives for the wrong tank connection. Best action? A) stop unloading until chemical/tank/connection are verified B) unload quickly C) dilute with water D) let driver decide alone
22. Hypochlorite mixed with acid can release: A) chlorine gas B) oxygen only C) sand D) fluoride
23. A caustic feed pump sticks on and pH rises rapidly. Best response? A) isolate/control feed, verify pH, protect treatment/distribution, and document B) ignore if water is clear C) add acid blindly without procedure D) disable pH analyzer
24. A coagulant feed failure during high turbidity can quickly affect: A) clarification/filter performance and finished water turbidity B) customer billing only C) fluoride MCL D) tank coating
25. A filter is producing acceptable turbidity but particle counts are rising. Best T5 interpretation? A) early warning may indicate performance change before turbidity limit B) particle counts never matter C) filter is perfect D) no need to investigate
26. A membrane integrity failure indicates: A) potential barrier compromise B) stronger chlorine C) lower PFAS D) more alkalinity
27. RO treatment for PFAS requires planning for: A) concentrate handling B) chlorine residual only C) hydrant flushing D) jar-test paddles
28. GAC breakthrough means: A) contaminant is passing through as media capacity is exhausted B) media is brand new C) pH is too low only D) tank has air gap
29. Ion exchange resin exhaustion can cause: A) target contaminant leakage/breakthrough B) instant disinfection C) more CT D) lower water age
30. A lab reports an unexpected contaminant detection just above an MCL. First step? A) verify result, sample details, compliance basis, and required response B) publicly announce without review C) delete sample D) ignore unless customer complains
31. A monitoring violation means: A) required monitoring/reporting was missed or invalid B) contaminant always exceeded health limit C) pump failed D) source dried up
32. A treatment technique violation means: A) required process/control was not met B) only a customer complaint C) only a late bill D) all water is automatically safe
33. Public notification timing depends mainly on: A) risk/violation tier and regulatory requirements B) operator preference C) tank size D) weather
34. A Tier 1 situation generally indicates: A) urgent potential health risk requiring rapid notice B) annual routine notice only C) no health concern D) billing update
35. A Consumer Confidence Report is: A) annual customer water quality report B) daily pump log C) filter inspection sheet D) chemical SDS
36. A SCADA trend shows pump amps slowly increasing over weeks. Best use of this trend? A) plan inspection/maintenance before failure B) ignore until trip C) delete history D) increase chlorine
37. Pump cavitation can damage: A) impellers and pump components B) sample bottles C) CCRs D) coagulant only
38. Water hammer can be reduced by: A) controlled valve operation, surge protection, and pump control B) faster valve closures C) ignoring air valves D) lowering all residual
39. Lockout/tagout protects workers from: A) hazardous energy B) nitrate C) microplastics D) taste and odor
40. Confined-space entry should occur only after: A) hazard evaluation, atmospheric testing, controls, and permit/procedure as applicable B) a quick look C) no paperwork D) lone entry
41. A good emergency response plan includes: A) roles, contacts, alternatives, communication, and exercises B) only old phone numbers C) no public communication D) no maps
42. Mutual aid is useful because it can provide: A) staff, equipment, supplies, or operational support during emergencies B) automatic MCL waivers C) no documentation D) lower DBPs directly
43. A vulnerability assessment helps identify: A) security and operational weaknesses B) finished pH C) fluoride dose D) filter loading only
44. Backflow risk is highest where: A) a cross-connection exists with pressure reversal potential B) only clean water exists C) no plumbing exists D) tanks are full
45. An air gap protects by: A) physical separation between potable and nonpotable sources B) chemical reaction C) UV light D) pH buffering
46. Lead and copper compliance sampling focuses on: A) customer taps/premise plumbing under specified conditions B) raw water only C) filter backwash only D) hydrants only
47. Corrosion control changes can affect: A) lead/copper release and distribution stability B) only tank level C) only source yield D) only pump horsepower
48. If a process change improves turbidity but increases DBP formation potential, best response? A) optimize the full treatment train rather than one metric alone B) keep change forever C) ignore DBPs D) stop monitoring
49. T5-level leadership means: A) anticipating system-wide consequences and directing safe, compliant response B) only doing single calculations C) avoiding communication D) waiting for others only
50. In a serious treatment upset, the final priority should be: A) public health and compliance B) production convenience C) avoiding paperwork D) keeping alarms quiet
