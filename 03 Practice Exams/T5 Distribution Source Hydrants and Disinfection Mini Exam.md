# T5 Distribution Source Hydrants and Disinfection Mini Exam

> Original study questions. Not official California State exam questions. These questions are source-supported but still caveated pending final source-depth review.

## Questions

1. What is the main purpose of a drinking water distribution system? A) Deliver potable water to customers while maintaining pressure, flow, and water quality B) Replace all treatment C) Eliminate all sampling D) Store untreated wastewater
2. Which list best represents distribution-system components? A) Mains, valves, hydrants, storage, pumps, meters, and services B) Only lab incubators C) Only source-water intakes D) Only jar-test paddles
3. Why are looped distribution systems generally preferred over dead ends? A) They improve circulation, redundancy, and operational flexibility B) They remove the need for valves C) They eliminate disinfection D) They prevent all pipe breaks
4. What water-quality issue is common in dead ends or low-flow areas? A) Increased water age and residual loss B) Increased membrane salt rejection C) No need for flushing D) Elimination of sediment
5. What does AWWA G200 primarily support in this vault? A) Distribution-system operation and management concepts B) PFAS federal MCL values C) Lab incubation only D) Rainfall maps only
6. What is a key caveat for AWWA standards? A) They are not complete specifications and do not supersede law or code B) They replace all state rules C) They are quiz sites D) They prohibit local requirements
7. What are transmission lines? A) Larger pipelines that move water between major system components B) Lab glassware C) Turbidity standards only D) Chlorine tablets only
8. Why does pipe material selection matter? A) It affects pressure capacity, corrosion, installation, water quality, and service life B) It only affects color C) It replaces hydraulic design D) It eliminates maintenance
9. What does pipe C value relate to? A) Hydraulic roughness/friction in flow calculations B) Calcium hardness only C) Coliform results only D) Chloramine breakpoint only
10. What is one purpose of distribution storage? A) Balance demand and support pressure/emergency supply B) Replace all pumps C) Store sewage D) Eliminate residual monitoring
11. Why are valves important in distribution? A) Isolation, control, flushing, maintenance, and emergency response B) They replace all hydrants C) They eliminate all water age D) They are only used in labs
12. What is the primary purpose of a fire hydrant? A) Public fire protection service B) Chemical coagulation C) Incubation D) Fluoride analysis
13. Which hydrant type is intended for freezing climates? A) Dry-barrel fire hydrant B) Wet-only hydrant C) Lab hydrant D) Membrane hydrant
14. Which AWWA standard covers dry-barrel fire hydrants? A) ANSI/AWWA C502 B) ANSI/AWWA B600 C) ANSI/AWWA B112 D) ANSI/AWWA C510
15. Why should non-fire hydrant use be restricted and controlled? A) To maintain hydrants for fire emergencies and protect water quality B) To remove chlorine C) To eliminate records D) To avoid valves
16. If a hydrant cannot be opened or closed by one person using a proper hydrant wrench, what is the best response? A) Repair it instead of forcing it B) Force it C) Ignore it D) Remove all hydrants
17. Why can using a hydrant to dispense drinking water require special care? A) Downstream surfaces may need disinfection and material-suitability evaluation B) Hydrants sterilize automatically C) Water never contacts surfaces D) Hydrants eliminate risk
18. What AWWA manual is commonly paired with hydrant maintenance topics? A) AWWA M17 B) AWWA B403 C) AWWA B600 D) AWWA B114
19. Why is distribution-system disinfection important? A) Distribution deficiencies can contribute to contamination risk B) It removes treatment C) It eliminates sampling D) It replaces pressure
20. What is a common contamination source during water main installation or repair? A) Soil, sediment, trench water, or foreign water entering pipe/appurtenances B) CCR wording C) UV serial number D) Spreadsheet formatting
21. Which sequence best fits pipeline disinfection work? A) Inspection, sanitary construction, flushing/cleaning, disinfection, final flushing, bacteriological testing, connection, documentation B) Skip inspection C) Paint pipe only D) Jar test only
22. What are common storage-facility contamination routes? A) Open/missing vents, hatches, roof openings, inspection activities, and foreign matter B) Sealed hatches only C) Clean sample bottles only D) pH meters only
23. Which AWWA standards are commonly tied to water main and storage disinfection? A) C651 and C652 B) B112 and B114 only C) B600 and B604 only D) C502 and C510 only
24. Why is bacteriological testing part of disinfection work? A) It helps verify sanitary conditions before return to potable service B) It replaces disinfection C) It measures pipe C value D) It confirms paint color
25. Why may dechlorination be needed after disinfection? A) Highly chlorinated water may need treatment before discharge B) Chlorine disappears instantly C) It increases pipe roughness D) It replaces sampling
26. Which chemicals are commonly used for pipeline/storage disinfection? A) Liquid chlorine, sodium hypochlorite, and calcium hypochlorite B) Only alum C) Only carbon D) Only table salt
27. Which free-chlorine species is more effective as a disinfectant? A) Hypochlorous acid B) Hypochlorite ion C) Calcium carbonate D) Nitrate
28. What happens to chlorine effectiveness as pH rises? A) More free chlorine shifts toward less-effective hypochlorite ion B) Chlorine becomes more acidic C) Chlorine becomes PFAS D) Chlorine becomes turbidity
29. What does AWWA M20 support? A) Chlorination/chloramination practice, chemistry, equipment, safety, strategy, and residual testing B) Only fire hose threads C) Only wells D) Only jar-test math
30. What does AWWA B403 cover? A) Aluminum sulfate B) Fire hydrants C) Membrane systems D) Cross-connection assemblies
31. What is aluminum sulfate commonly used for? A) Coagulation B) Hydrant operation C) Fire-flow testing only D) Backflow testing only
32. What does AWWA B600 cover? A) Powdered activated carbon B) Dry-barrel hydrants C) RO/NF systems D) Aluminum sulfate
33. What does AWWA B604 cover? A) Granular activated carbon B) PFAS timing only C) Program management only D) Hydrant color only
34. What is activated carbon commonly used for? A) Adsorbing taste-and-odor compounds and some organic contaminants B) Raising all residuals C) Replacing hydrants D) Measuring pipe wall thickness
35. What does AWWA B112 cover? A) Microfiltration and ultrafiltration membrane systems B) Dry-barrel hydrants C) Aluminum sulfate D) Double-check assemblies
36. What does AWWA B114 cover? A) Reverse osmosis and nanofiltration systems B) Hydrant color only C) Water main disinfection only D) Powdered carbon only
37. Which membrane group generally removes dissolved material better? A) RO/NF B) MF/UF C) Fire hydrants D) Dead-end mains
38. Why is the uploaded Water Sources file caveated? A) It has valid AWWA front matter but also suspicious non-book/unrelated parsed text B) It is blank C) It is only a hydrant standard D) It is official California regulation
39. Product standards should be paired with operator/training references because: A) Product standards do not fully explain operations, troubleshooting, or site-specific design B) Product standards are never useful C) Operator references override all laws D) Site conditions never matter
40. What controls final installation, operation, and compliance decisions? A) Current regulations, state/local rules, authority having jurisdiction, engineering design, manufacturer instructions, and utility SOPs B) Flashcards only C) A single old workbook only D) The shortest answer choice
