26. A high settled-water turbidity leaving sedimentation often increases loading on: A) filters B) billing software C) fluoride tanks D) distribution maps
27. A sudden filter effluent turbidity spike can indicate: A) filter breakthrough B) lower demand C) perfect coagulation D) lower particle loading
28. The main purpose of filter backwashing is to: A) add chlorine B) remove accumulated solids and restore headloss C) raise alkalinity D) reduce pump speed
29. Disinfection byproducts generally increase when disinfectant reacts with: A) natural organic matter B) sodium chloride only C) nitrogen gas D) inert sand
30. Chloramination is commonly used to: A) maintain longer-lasting residual B) remove all PFAS C) eliminate corrosion D) replace filtration
31. Free chlorine residual is usually more reactive than: A) chloramine residual B) dissolved oxygen C) alkalinity D) hardness
32. A cross-connection program is intended to prevent: A) overbilling B) backflow and contamination C) pump cavitation only D) hydrant painting defects
33. Backpressure backflow can occur when downstream pressure is: A) lower than supply pressure B) higher than supply pressure C) zero D) unrelated to pressure
34. Backsiphonage is commonly associated with: A) negative or reduced supply pressure B) excessive source nitrate C) high hardness D) filter ripening
35. A boil water notice is most directly related to: A) possible microbial risk B) high electric use C) pump efficiency D) low aesthetic color only
36. A loss of positive pressure in distribution is concerning because it can: A) allow intrusion B) reduce water age only C) improve CT D) remove DBPs
37. The Revised Total Coliform Rule uses coliform detections to trigger: A) assessment and corrective action B) fluoride feed C) meter replacement D) filter-to-waste only
