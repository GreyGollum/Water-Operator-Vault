# T5 Lab Procedures and Methods Mini Exam

> Original study questions for operator-level lab procedures and method recognition. Not official State exam questions. Compliance claims require CFR/EPA/state crosscheck before public release.

## Questions

1. For drinking-water compliance monitoring, which source has the highest authority? A) Current CFR and incorporated approved method text B) Manufacturer catalog C) Operator study note D) Informal training handout
2. Under routine RTCR total coliform/E. coli monitoring, the standard sample volume is usually: A) 100 mL B) 1 mL C) 10 L D) 500 mL
3. For RTCR routine samples, presence/absence testing is generally: A) Sufficient for total coliform/E. coli compliance B) Never allowed C) Only used for turbidity D) Used only for metals
4. Why is sodium thiosulfate used in microbiology sample bottles for chlorinated water? A) To neutralize chlorine residual B) To increase pH to 14 C) To add nutrients for algae D) To preserve metals
5. A collection-to-incubation time beyond the approved holding window can: A) Make a microbiological result questionable or invalid B) Improve sample reliability C) Convert CFU to MPN automatically D) Remove the need for documentation
6. CFU stands for: A) Colony-forming units B) Chlorine formation units C) Certified fluoride uptake D) Carbon filtration units
7. MPN stands for: A) Most probable number B) Maximum pressure neutralization C) Minimum particle number D) Meter probe normalization
8. HPC is best described as: A) A general heterotrophic bacterial growth indicator B) The same thing as an E. coli MCL C) A metal digestion method D) A chlorine dose calculation
9. Which is an HPC method family captured in the lab section? A) Pour plate / plate count B) Ion exchange softening C) Lime-soda coagulation D) Fluoride MCL averaging
10. Precision describes: A) Reproducibility of repeated results B) Closeness to the true value only C) The sample bottle size D) The age of the instrument
11. Accuracy describes: A) Closeness to the true or accepted value B) How many samples are shipped C) The color of a reagent D) Whether a sample is groundwater
12. Can results be precise but inaccurate? A) Yes B) No C) Only if pH is 7 D) Only for turbidity
13. A blank is used mainly to check: A) Contamination or background contribution B) Pump efficiency C) Filter loading rate D) Distribution pressure
14. A duplicate or replicate analysis is used mainly to check: A) Precision/repeatability B) Lead service line inventory C) CT table compliance D) Tank overflow elevation
15. A spike or known addition is used mainly to check: A) Accuracy/recovery B) Pipe age C) Valve direction D) Floc blanket depth
16. Chain of custody is important because it: A) Preserves sample traceability B) Raises chlorine residual C) Lowers turbidity D) Replaces calibration
17. An analytical standard is used to: A) Calibrate or check a method B) Clean the sink drain C) Store waste acid D) Increase sample hold time
18. Reagents should generally not be used past expiration because: A) Strength or purity may change B) They become official regulations C) They stop needing labels D) They always become harmless
19. Reagent water quality matters because: A) It can add background or interfere with analyses B) It only affects taste panels C) It replaces QA/QC D) It is used only for fire protection
20. The difference between concentration and acid/base strength is that: A) Concentration is amount present; strength is degree of ionization B) They are identical terms C) Strength means bottle size D) Concentration means sample temperature only
21. Acids and bases should be stored separately because: A) They can react and release heat B) They become more accurate together C) They are both sample preservatives only D) They cancel documentation requirements
22. Oxidizers and reducers should be stored separately because: A) They can react vigorously B) They form perfect blanks C) They lower sample temperature D) They replace fume hoods
23. Beaker graduations are generally: A) Approximate B) More accurate than Class A pipets C) Used as legal standards D) Always NIST-certified
24. A buret is commonly used to: A) Deliver measured titrant in titration B) Incubate microbiology samples C) Measure pipe flow directly D) Store chlorine gas
25. A meniscus reading error affects: A) Volume measurement B) Pump horsepower only C) Operator certification only D) Filter media size only
26. Analytical balances should be protected from drafts and vibration because: A) These can affect mass readings B) Drafts sterilize samples C) Vibration improves accuracy D) Balances are only decorative
27. A titration endpoint is: A) The method-defined point where the reaction is considered complete B) The highest tank level C) The last sample of the month D) The instrument serial number
28. A colorimetric test generally estimates concentration by: A) Color intensity from a chemical reaction B) Pipe diameter C) Pump speed D) Valve torque
29. A turbidimetric method is based on: A) Cloudiness or light scatter B) Total organic carbon only C) Valve position D) Taste threshold
30. pH and ion-selective electrodes require: A) Proper calibration and stable readings B) No standards ever C) Chlorine gas feed D) Hot lime softening
31. Hardness is commonly associated with which method family in operator training? A) EDTA titration B) UV disinfection only C) Cryptosporidium microscopy only D) Ozone CT only
32. Alkalinity is commonly associated with: A) Titration-based methods B) Distribution flushing only C) Virus enumeration only D) Backflow device testing only
33. Residual chlorine testing may use: A) DPD colorimetric methods or amperometric titration, depending on approved context B) Only a balance C) Only a microscope D) Only a jar test paddle
34. Turbidity is commonly measured by: A) Nephelometric methods B) EDTA hardness titration C) Colony counts D) Sodium thiosulfate blanks only
35. Dissolved oxygen in older wet-chemistry training is associated with: A) Winkler method concepts B) Lead and copper tap sampling only C) PFAS hazard index only D) Fluoride secondary standard only
36. Nitrate/nitrite and phosphate are often associated with: A) Colorimetric nutrient method families B) Valve exercising C) Backwash rate only D) Pressure-zone maps only
37. A certified laboratory SOP should be treated as: A) Required operating direction for that lab/method B) Optional decoration C) Less important than a quiz card D) A substitute for sample labeling
38. If an unusual lab result is received, the operator should first: A) Verify sample identity/result status and follow SOP/supervisor/regulatory path B) Ignore it until next month C) Change the result to expected value D) Dispose of all records
39. Why should lab method versions matter? A) Approval can depend on exact method version/date B) Versions never matter C) Versions only affect font size D) Versions replace QA/QC
40. What should be done before using a training-reference method as a compliance method? A) Crosscheck against CFR/EPA/state approved methods B) Assume it is approved forever C) Remove the method number D) Skip certification requirements
41. The WEF lab techniques reference is best used as: A) A training/reference source for lab fundamentals B) The controlling drinking-water legal authority C) A replacement for 40 CFR Part 141 D) A substitute for SDS
42. The Water and Wastewater Examination Manual is useful mainly for: A) Method-family awareness and QA/QC training B) Current California legal text C) Replacing Standard Methods entirely D) Setting PFAS MCLs
43. The uploaded Water Quality Concepts files should be used carefully because: A) File-integrity issues were observed B) They are official California Title 22 text C) They replace all EPA methods D) They contain no sampling topics
44. Which item belongs in lab QA/QC records? A) Calibration/check results B) Operator lunch schedule only C) Pump paint color D) Tree trimming schedule
45. What is a common reason for using certified standards or certificates of analysis? A) Traceability and known quality B) To avoid calibration C) To eliminate hold times D) To prove a sample was not collected
46. What is a key reason not to copy full method procedures into the vault? A) Copyright/SOP concerns and need to keep study notes at operator-summary level B) Operators cannot learn concepts C) Method names are unimportant D) Flashcards require legal text only
47. Which source layer is strongest for drinking-water compliance method approval? A) CFR / incorporated approved method B) Social media summary C) Unverified quiz site D) A handwritten shortcut with no source
48. What does “method recognition” mean in this vault? A) Knowing what a method family is used for and its limits without replacing SOPs B) Memorizing every procedural step verbatim C) Ignoring approved-method versions D) Using any kit for any analyte
49. Why should water and wastewater method cards be tagged carefully? A) Some methods/authorities differ between drinking-water compliance and wastewater permit testing B) Tags are only decorative C) Wastewater methods automatically set drinking-water MCLs D) Drinking-water samples never need labs
50. Before public release, lab-method cards should be: A) Crosschecked against EPA/CFR/state sources and caveated where needed B) Published without review C) Stripped of source IDs D) Mixed with unsourced exam dumps
