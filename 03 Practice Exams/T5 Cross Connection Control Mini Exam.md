# T5 Cross Connection Control Mini Exam

> Original study questions. Not official State exam questions. Use local program rules and approved tester references for actual installation and testing.

## Questions

1. What is a cross-connection? A) A physical or potential connection between potable water and a source of unknown or lesser quality B) A filter-to-waste valve C) A chlorine sample point only D) A finished-water storage tank
2. What is backflow? A) Unwanted reverse flow into the potable water system B) Normal finished-water flow C) Filtered water entering a clearwell D) Routine hydrant flushing
3. Backsiphonage is caused by: A) Reduced supply pressure B) Higher downstream pressure C) High fluoride residual D) Low hardness
4. Backpressure is caused by: A) Downstream pressure greater than supply pressure B) Reduced supply pressure only C) Low turbidity D) A sample tap
5. Which method provides the strongest physical separation? A) Air gap B) Single check valve C) Plain hose bibb D) Unlisted adapter
6. What does RP stand for? A) Reduced-pressure principle assembly B) Reverse pump C) Residual pressure pipe D) Routine pressure
7. What does DC stand for in this context? A) Double check valve assembly B) Distribution chlorine C) Direct current D) Daily calculation
8. A PVB protects against: A) Backsiphonage only B) Backpressure only C) Pump cavitation D) Radionuclides only
9. An AVB is generally not used under: A) Continuous pressure B) Atmospheric conditions C) Point-of-use conditions D) Open discharge conditions
10. A dual check is: A) Not the same as a testable DC assembly B) Always the same as an RP assembly C) The highest physical separation D) A turbidity meter part
11. What is containment protection? A) Protection at the service connection B) Protection only inside a single faucet C) A jar test method D) A chlorine demand test
12. What is isolation protection? A) Protection at or near the internal hazard B) Only protection at the water source C) Only a billing meter D) Only a tank screen
13. AWWA C510 covers: A) Double check valve backflow prevention assemblies B) All field-test procedures C) All California rules D) All wastewater lab methods
14. A complete DC assembly under AWWA C510 includes: A) Two independent check valves, two shutoff valves, and four test cocks B) One check valve and no test cocks C) Only a vacuum breaker D) Only an air gap
15. AWWA C510 is best used for: A) DC assembly construction/design study points B) Replacing local rules C) Replacing tester procedures D) Setting PFAS MCLs
16. Why must a testable assembly be accessible? A) It must be tested, maintained, repaired, or replaced B) It must be hidden C) It must always be buried D) It must be painted
17. Why does RP drainage matter? A) The relief valve may discharge and needs a safe location B) RP assemblies never discharge C) Drainage proves pH compliance D) Drainage replaces testing
18. What can an unprotected bypass do? A) Defeat the intended protection B) Improve testing automatically C) Replace the assembly D) Eliminate records
19. Who controls actual approved installation and testing requirements? A) Authority having jurisdiction and program rules B) A random online quiz C) Product catalog only D) Handwritten shortcut only
20. Which type protects against backsiphonage only and not backpressure? A) PVB B) RP C) DC D) Air gap
21. Which type is commonly associated with high-hazard protection where approved? A) RP assembly B) Single check valve C) Pipe coupling D) Hose adapter only
22. Which type is commonly limited to lower-hazard use where approved? A) DC assembly B) Unprotected bypass C) Single check only D) Missing air gap
23. What should be recorded for an assembly program? A) Type, size, model, serial number, location, test date, result, and tester information B) Paint color only C) Weather only D) Customer name only
24. What does a flow arrow show? A) Required direction of flow B) Pipe age C) Pump horsepower D) Meter reading date
25. Which source is still needed for detailed field-test procedures? A) USC Foundation Manual or accepted local tester reference B) PFAS brochure C) Fluoride MCL table D) Taste chart
26. Why is hazard level important? A) It determines the level of protection needed B) It only determines pipe color C) It removes local authority requirements D) It replaces testing
27. Which is true about AWWA C510? A) It is a standard for DC assemblies, not a complete program manual B) It is Title 22 C) It is the USC field-test manual D) It is a PFAS rule
28. Which item is usually part of a testable assembly? A) Test cocks B) Jar-test paddle C) Fluoride probe only D) Sample incubator
29. What should happen when local rules are more specific than a study note? A) Follow the local rules B) Ignore them C) Use oldest note only D) Use shortest answer
30. What is the best study caveat? A) Study aids do not replace approved program rules or tester procedures B) They replace all manuals C) They are official exam questions D) They eliminate testing requirements
